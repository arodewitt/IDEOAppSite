Catalog of Scribbles

Posters

Digitally printed, 11x14in

Last semester, as I started to think about my MFA thesis, I wanted to reduce the themes in my work to their simplest expression. I came up with these posters styled in the taxonomical rigor of 19th century natural history, but with playful content.

Natural science illustrations command an inherent authority, and I find myself often using this aesthetic to tell stories. But of course that authority isn’t always earned. This time, I started creating forms that would resist order; just pages and pages of scribbles. Then I put them into a setting that takes them very seriously, hopefully showing that it’s a little absurd to think exact classification schemes can make sense of everything.
